Why I Built Peak Task

Climbing the mountain that is life, one task at a time

David Raji · January 2026

I had a problem.

My to-do list was 74 items long. Every morning I'd open it and feel overwhelmed. I'd pick something. Anything. Just to feel productive.

But I wasn't doing the most important thing.

I was busy, but not effective.

So I started looking for a to-do app that could automatically rank my tasks. I tried lots of different systems but they weren't intuitive. None of them worked the way I needed. Just an input of the importance and duration.

So, I decided to try to build it myself.

Although ranking tasks was a good start, I needed something else to stay motivated. That's when I had the idea: climbing the mountain that is life.

Every completed Peak Task would add to your height. You'd start at ground level. But over time, you'd ascend. First to hills. Then to cliffs. Eventually to mountains. Real places in the world.

So I learned Flutter. I started simple. A list. A ranking algorithm. A stopwatch that tracked your height.

I showed it to friends. They had ideas for shared lists, widgets, and more. Each iteration made it better.

The result is Peak Task.
